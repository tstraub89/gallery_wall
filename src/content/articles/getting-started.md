## Step 1: Set Up Your Wall (30 seconds)

When you first open [GalleryPlanner](/), you'll see the Wall Configuration panel.

1. Enter your wall **width** (e.g., 96 inches for an 8-foot wall)
2. Enter your wall **height** (e.g., 72 inches for 6 feet)
3. Click **"Apply"**

**Pro tip:** Measure the actual area where you want frames, not your entire wall. If you're planning a gallery wall above a sofa, measure from the top of the sofa to the ceiling.

**Not sure about dimensions?** Start with a standard 96" Ã— 72" wall to experimentâ€”you can always adjust later.

## Step 2: Add Frames to Your Library (1 minute)

Now it's time to add the frames you own (or want to buy).

### Using Standard Sizes

Most frames come in standard sizes. Here's how to add them:

1. Open the **Library** panel (left sidebar on desktop, bottom toolbar on mobile)
2. Browse the standard sizes: 4x6, 5x7, 8x10, 11x14, 16x20, etc.
3. Click the **"+"** button next to each size you have
4. Adjust the **count** if you have multiples (e.g., "I have 5 8x10 frames")

### Creating Custom Frames

Have an unusual frame size?

1. Click **"Custom Frame"** in the Library
2. Enter the exact width and height (measure the outer edges of the frame)
3. Choose a frame color (black, white, wood, gold, or custom)
4. Click **"Add to Library"**

## Step 3: Design Your Layout (2 minutes)

Now for the fun partâ€”arranging your frames!

### Option A: Let AI Do It (Easiest)

1. Click **"Smart Layout"** in the toolbar
2. Select **"Auto"** (recommended) or choose a specific style:
   - **Grid:** Clean, organized rows and columns
   - **Masonry:** Staggered, brick-like pattern
   - **Skyline:** Frames align at top or bottom
3. Click **"Generate"**

In seconds, GalleryPlanner will create a professional layout using your frame inventory. Don't love it? Click "Generate" again for a new arrangement.



### Option B: Design Manually (More Control)

Prefer a hands-on approach?

1. **Drag frames** from your library onto the canvas
2. **Position them** where you want using click-and-drag
3. Use **alignment guides** (they appear automatically) to keep frames evenly spaced
4. **Rotate frames** by clicking the rotation icon

**Pro tip:** Start with your largest frame firstâ€”place it where you want the focal point, then build around it.

## Step 4: Customize Your Design (1 minute)

Once your frames are on the wall, fine-tune the details.

### Adjust Frame Properties

Click any frame on your canvas to open the **Properties Panel**:

- **Position:** See exact X/Y coordinates
- **Size:** Width and height (custom frames can be resized)
- **Rotation:** Rotate to landscape or portrait
- **Frame Color:** Change the color even after placement
- **Matting:** Toggle matting on/off

### Add Photos (Optional)

Want to see what your gallery wall will look like with actual photos?

1. Select a frame on the canvas
2. Click **"Add Photo"** in the Properties panel
3. Choose a photo from your device
4. The photo fills the frame automatically

**Privacy note:** Your photos stay on your deviceâ€”they're never uploaded to a server. [Learn more](/privacy).

## Troubleshooting: First-Timer FAQs

**"I don't see my frames on the canvas after adding them to the library."**

â†’ You need to *drag* frames from the library onto the canvas. Adding them to the library just makes them availableâ€”placement is a separate step.

**"Smart Layout says 'Not enough frames.'"**

â†’ You need at least 3-4 frames in your library for Smart Layout to work. Add more frames to your inventory.

**"I can't move my frame exactly where I want it."**

â†’ Frames snap to a grid by default for alignment. Hold `Shift` while dragging for free movement, or disable snap-to-grid in Settings.
